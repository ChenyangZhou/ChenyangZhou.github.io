Hierarchical Monte Carlo Tree Search for the planning of cooperative driving maneuvers

Master Thesis at Forschungszentrum Informatik, Karlsruhe

We have developed a hierarchical MCTS algorithm to solve the cooperative learning and planning problems in the multi-agent system. Our algorithm is proved to converge to the global optimum under certain conditions. The effective search depth is significantly increased.

  • Start Date: June 2017
  • End Date: Jan. 2018
  • Category: Machine Learning, Autonomous Driving

Automated Recognition of Unjustified ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance System) Warnings

Part of my internship at Daimler AG

We developed a new method to extract information from the time series data based on idea of HoG-Vector in the machine vision field. It has been proved that our method can precisely extract the information which is essential to distinguish between True-Positive and False-Positive of the ADAS-warnings. This method can be used to evaluate the performance of ADAS systems automatically and guide the further development.

  • Start Date: Feb. 2017
  • End Date: May. 2017
  • Category: Data Mining, Autonomous Driving
